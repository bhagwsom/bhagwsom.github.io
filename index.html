    About Me.

    Hi, I am Somya! I am a student at the School of Information at the University of Michigan working toward a Bachelor of Science in Information (BSI). I am minoring in Science, Technology, and Society from the Residential College in the College of Literature, Science, and Arts because I am intrigued by the dynamic interaction between technology, information, and cultures.

    I am interested in UI/UX, digital marketing, information security, and data analysis. I have honed my technical skills in coding and statistics with my coursework at the School of Information and nurtured my cultural competence with Spanish communication coursework in addition to my blended South Asian-American ethnic background.

    I became better versed with my own ethnic cultural background as a student of Sangeeta Kar at the Sangeetayan Institute of Dance & Music of India for twelve years where I learned the oldest form of classical Indian dance called “Odissi” to communicate Indian cultural and mythological stories to audiences. Maturing as a global citizen by exposure to diverse friends from schools in the city of Saginaw, MI to the University of Michigan, I see the world enriched by our unique cultural backgrounds. However, I also see the risk of a cultural divide through miscommunications and inequitable access to information. My goal is to be part of the solution by increasing understanding among people by using the human connection.

    When not working, I love to travel, explore new places and create memories through photography. More than commemorating landmarks, I like to take my lens to local lives to capture their struggles and stories. Growing up in Saginaw, it surprised me that most friends my age were either oblivious to or just overlooked the economic and racial disparities which drew my attention. I dwelled on the disparate lives of people and realized then that success in business lies not just in making profit but in enhancing lives of people. I hope to create viable ways to connect all people with information technology and be at the forefront of what I see as a ‘techno-humanistic’ approach to business.

    At U of M, inspired by Brandon Stanton’s blogging methods in Humans of New York, I brought my love for storytelling and photography to create a blog called Students of Umich. It uses Instagram and Facebook to connect to students’ journeys as they chase their passions while enmeshed in university life. Instead of stories being passed down through lineages, technology allows stories to pass through networks of people. As people hear stories, they gain new perspectives and can participate as global citizens. They can commiserate with Syrian refugees and #MeToo marchers as well as rejoice with victims saved through miraculous relief efforts. People can live vicariously through others to understand their own unfathomable struggles often untouched by media.

    Always aiming to expand my limits, I also focus on personal goals such as fitness and meditation. I like to live my life like Walt Whitman’s quote “I believe that a leaf of grass is no less than the journey-work of the stars.” I aim to bring a more humanistic approach to Information and be a part of this new business era- an era in which business is poised to acquire a soul.

    Michigan Muscle Club: Bringing leadership, marketing, and inclusivity

    Project LifeSong

    In 2016, I had the opportunity to work with Irene Hensinger to be apart of founding a small non-profit in Saginaw, MI. The Lifesongs Project is a local volunteer effort to provide the memory-impaired with an IPod programmed with a personalized music playlist to improve communication and quality of life through music-triggered memories. Though this project is small, it will improve lives of memory-impaired patients. My role through this group was both to install software, compile databases or music, and publicize the organization locally to raise money.
